# Provenance

## Character origin

K was developed through iterative human-directed character exploration in July 2026. The final open-source specification uses a project-owned generated reference selected and revised through multiple human decisions. Third-party character files are not bundled or traced.

The public character is defined by this combination:

- soft, heavy, forward-leaning rounded cow body
- one long white eye window with two drooping black eyes
- two short curved horns and no animal ears
- warm-yellow over-ear headphones with a black band
- a tiny integrated muzzle with two nostril dots and a short mouth line
- clock-shaped tail
- spring-like legs and solid black short boots
- rough crayon and dry-pencil editorial texture

Generic ideas such as a simple mascot, stick limbs, headphones, a clock, or a geometric animal are not claimed as exclusive. The project claims only its own concrete assets and expression.

## AI-assisted process

AI image generation was used during ideation and production. The bundled raster reference is the selected official character source for this release. Rejected drafts and third-party reference files are excluded.

Future AI-assisted assets must record meaningful human choices and revisions. A one-click generation with only generic keywords is not sufficient provenance for an official asset.

## External projects

Other open-source illustration Skills were reviewed for packaging and quality-control practices. Their characters, prompt text, examples, metaphors, layouts, and visual assets are not included in this repository.

Ian's open-source project [Ian Xiaohei Illustrations](https://github.com/helloianneo/ian-xiaohei-illustrations) informed part of the content-to-visual workflow and Skill organization. 小K Skill was subsequently restructured for multi-platform publishing, with independently written instructions and an independently developed character, platform rules, prompt system, composition guidance, and visual assets.

## Release checklist

- search the repository for third-party names and copied fragments
- verify that every bundled image has a declared source and license
- compare new examples against the character DNA and prohibited patterns
- retain generation prompts and edit notes for official raster releases
- perform a fresh trademark and visual similarity search before commercial registration

The latest pre-release search is recorded in `SIMILARITY-REVIEW.md`.
